Life of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

A Journey of Wisdom Innovation, and Service to the Nation.

  • 1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1960: Graduated in Aerospace Engineering from Madras Institute of Techonology.
  • 1969: Joined ISRO and became Project for SLV-III.
  • 1998: Led India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests.
  • 2002-2007: Served as the 11th President of India.
  • 2015: Passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shilong.

Achievements of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Celebrating a Legacy of Brilliance and Dedications to India

  1. Played a major role in India's Civilian Space Program and Military Missile Development.
  2. Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the Development of Ballistic Missile and Launch Vehicle Technology.
  3. Directed the Project to Develop India's first Indigeneous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III)
  4. Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister (1992-1999)
  5. 11th President of India (2002-2007) and widely regrated as the "People President."
  6. Key figure in the 1998 Pokhan-II Nuclear Tests.
  7. Authored Inspiration Books like *Wings of Fire*, *Ignited Minds*, and *India 2020*.
  8. Received Prestigious Awards including Padma Bhushan (1981). Padma Vibhushan (1990), and Bharat Ratna (1990).
